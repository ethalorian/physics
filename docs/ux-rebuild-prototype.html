Acceleration & the kinematic equations

◷ ~18 min 62% complete

What acceleration really measures

Acceleration is the rate at which velocity changes. If a cart speeds up by the same amount every second, its acceleration is constant — and that single fact is what makes the kinematic equations work.

Whenever velocity changes by a steady amount, we can predict position and speed at any moment with three equations. The first connects velocity, time, and acceleration:

v = v0 + at

Here v₀ is the starting velocity, a is the acceleration, and t is the time elapsed. Notice it's just the definition of acceleration, rearranged.

Worked example

A cart starts at 2 m/s and accelerates at 3 m/s² for 4 seconds. How fast is it moving at the end?

GGiven
v₀ = 2 m/s  ·  a = 3 m/s²  ·  t = 4 s
EEquation
v = v₀ + at
WWork
v = 2 + (3)(4) = 2 + 12
AAnswer
v = 14 m/s
